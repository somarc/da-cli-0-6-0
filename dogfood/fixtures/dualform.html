SUBSTRATE · EXPRESSION · PROOF

One form. Two states.

Dualform is the dogfood lab for paired silhouette cutouts: one mass, two material truths, superimposed with a CSS mask. Structure remains; expression moves through it.

Drag on any form (or hover on desktop). On mobile, scroll also drifts the hole as the stage moves through the viewport.

MECHANISM · ORRERY · MAP

Clockwork becomes sky.

Substrate: bare bronze gear cage — pure mechanism, no ornament. Expression: the same silhouette as a celestial orrery — indigo constellation arcs, chartreuse star points, glass dials. Time-keeping mass remapped into navigation.

No organic growth. The dual-state is instrument → cosmos.

VESSEL · MERCURY · SEAM

Porcelain holds lightning.

Substrate: unglazed porcelain laboratory vessel — mass and void only. Expression: the same amphora mid-metamorphosis into liquid mercury and chrome, with gold kintsugi seams that refuse to erase the ceramic identity.

Dual-state is ceramic → metal-light, not garden overgrowth.

What Dualform proves

  • Silhouette lock. Each B plate was reference-edited from its A so contour, camera, and mass register.
  • Domain remap. Ledger / orrery / vessel show three different expression vocabularies on the same contract — not one moss recipe reused.
  • Progressive enhancement. Drag, hover, or scroll; reduced motion keeps a readable resting dual-state.
  • EDS-safe. LCP is a static image. Authors stack two images; decoration owns the stage.

How to author

In DA, use a dualform-hero block with two columns: copy left, art right. In the art cell, place substrate image first, then expression image second. That order is the contract.

Skill: eds-dualform — design intent, Imagine A→B prompts, block anatomy, media delivery.
