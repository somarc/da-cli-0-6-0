SUBSTRATE · EXPRESSION · PROOF

One form. Two states.

Dualform is the dogfood lab for paired silhouette cutouts: a bare structural ledger and its living dual-state, superimposed with a CSS mask. Structure remains; expression grows through it — the same contract as provenance on a proven site.

State A is substrate. State B is expression. The browser is the visualization layer — not a WebGL stage, not a slideshow. Scroll gently and the reveal deepens when motion is allowed.

What Dualform proves

  • Silhouette lock. State B was reference-edited from State A so contour, camera, and mass register. Mask quality cannot fix drift.
  • Static first. With JavaScript disabled, the soft diagonal wipe still reads as one object with two materials.
  • Progressive reveal. When motion is allowed, intersection depth gently increases how much of B is visible — never scroll-jack.
  • EDS-safe. LCP is the substrate image. No canvas. Authors stack two images in a table; decoration owns the stage.

How to author

In DA, use a dualform-hero block with two columns: copy left, art right. In the art cell, place substrate image first, then expression image second. That order is the contract.

Skill: eds-dualform — design intent, Imagine A→B prompts, block anatomy, media delivery.
