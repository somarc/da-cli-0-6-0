f022 · jobs, durability · fixed

Durable jobs weren't — a SIGKILLed runner's lock made resume crash

Symptom

Wave 4's core promise is "durable job resumes after interruption." The drill: start a 21-task job, SIGKILL the runner at 7/21, resume. Reality: the killed runner's lock directory survived it, and the resume crashed with a raw EEXIST — the one scenario durable jobs exist for was the one scenario they couldn't handle. The job state itself was intact; only the lock lied about a runner that no longer existed.

Why it matters

Interruption is not an edge case for agents — closed laptops, killed shells, and timeouts are the normal texture of agentic work. A durability primitive that requires a human to know "go delete the lock dir" fails exactly when it's needed. Critical for Wave 4: without this fix, the wave's proof line was false.

Resolution

Fixed in da-cli: claimJobRunner now probes the recorded owner pid for liveness. Provably dead (ESRCH) or missing metadata → the lock is stale and is reclaimed with a note. Live process or EPERM (can't prove death) → still refused, with the owner pid named. Field proof: SIGKILL at 7/21 → resume → 21/21 completed, every task attempts: 1 — no rework, no double-processing.

Lock

Liveness locks in jobs.test.js: a dead-pid lock is reclaimed; a live-pid lock (the test's own process.pid — guaranteed alive, not an arbitrary number) is refused with the exact collision message. Evidence: dogfood/evidence/wave-4/job-after-sigkill.json, job-resume-completed.json, job-tasks-final.json.
