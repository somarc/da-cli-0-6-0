f021 · bulk, jobs, contract · fixed

Bulk jobs reported success while doing nothing — and the fix for f016 was itself wrong

Symptom

Found while publishing the f015–f020 learning pages (this very site section): preview pages --bulk submitted cleanly, the job reached stopped — and processed zero paths. #bulkBody reused helixPath(), a URL-segment transform that strips the leading slash; the platform silently drops slash-less body paths, so the job "succeeded" with total: 0. Separately, the details poll crashed with a 404: the job route needs a ref segment — /job/{org}/{repo}/{ref}/{topic}/{name}/details — which f016's fix, verified against source rather than a live response, had omitted.

Why it matters

False success is the worst contract violation — worse than any error, because nothing tells you to look. And f021 corrects f016's correction: reading platform source produced a route that never matched a real job. The lesson is method, not just code: ground truth is a live response, not a source read — the links.self on an actual submission settled in one request what source archaeology got wrong. Bonus cascade: the ref-less route also explains why index build --wait believed its job was "transient, not queryable" — those jobs were queryable all along.

Resolution

Fixed in da-cli: bulk body paths are site-absolute (.html-stripped, leading slash kept); helixJob includes the ref. Field proof, same 7-path run: total: 0 + crash → 7/7 success, failed: 0, one parseable envelope — and index build --wait now polls its real job (38/38 processed) instead of falling back.

Lock

Four locks that had encoded the wrong shapes were flipped: bulk bodies assert /index-style paths; job-route tests assert the ref-ful shape. Tracked residue: publish pages accepts one source where preview pages is variadic (asymmetry), and the bulk poll crash surfaced as a bare stack trace under --format json — both queued with the f017 remainder.
