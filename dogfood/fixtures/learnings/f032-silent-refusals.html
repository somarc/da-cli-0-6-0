f032 · contract, safety · fixed

refusals are results — three commands said no with empty stdout

Symptom

Three field specimens in a single Wave 5 day, all under --format json: the f019 config-derived-target safety refusal (code sync, then preview page) and the DA structure validation error (content put on body-only HTML) each exited 1 with prose on stderr and nothing on stdout. The contract channel went silent exactly when the CLI was doing something right — refusing safely.

Why it matters

A refusal IS a result. An agent watching stdout sees an empty string and a nonzero exit — indistinguishable from a crash — when the actual situation is "reviewed, refused, and here is precisely how to proceed." The f019 tiers and validation gates are the CLI's best safety behavior; hiding their reasoning from the machine channel undoes half their value. This is the pre-dispatch cousin of f017's bootstrap seam, and the sixth and seventh envelope-march specimens.

Resolution

Fixed in da-cli: one seam in prepareWrite — safety refusals emit an ok:false envelope carrying the same reason as the prose plus an executable retry (da --org … --repo … --commit <operation>). The two content put/put-tree validation sites emit envelopes with the --wrap recovery. Stderr prose unchanged for humans. Field proof: the exact preview page refusal from the C1 drill now parses as one envelope with target, reason, and retry.

Lock

Two hermetic CLI locks in content-envelope.test.js: the config-derived-target refusal envelope (global-config fixture, preview tier) and the structure validation envelope (DA-backed fstab.yaml fixture, body-only HTML → --wrap in next[]). Both verified failing on old code; 609/609. Residue tracked: checkTopLevelBlocks --strict-sections exit and site freshness bare-array output remain on the march list.
