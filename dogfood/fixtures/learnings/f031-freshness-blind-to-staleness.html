f031 · site, freshness · fixed

site freshness compared the preview against itself — staleness was undetectable by construction

Symptom

Wave 5 drill C1 (stale preview/live): source re-put minutes after its preview, and site freshness said previewFreshness: fresh — repeatedly, with the source provably newer. The row's sourceLastModified was a timestamp from preview time, not the current source. Two independent ground-truth probes (CLI content list and raw DA /list) both showed the newer timestamp the freshness report refused to see.

Why it matters

Chain of two silent failures: DA /list reports lastModified as epoch milliseconds (a number); Date.parse(number) is NaN, so every DA source timestamp normalized to '' — and the row builder then silently substituted the helix-admin status's sourceLastModified, which is the source as of the last preview. Net effect: the tool compared the preview against itself and could never say preview-stale — the one verdict it exists to produce. The unit fixture modeled DA timestamps as strings, so the lock asserted the wrong platform shape (f021's lesson: ground truth is a live response, not an assumption).

Resolution

Fixed in da-cli: timestamp normalization handles numeric epochs (ms, tolerating seconds). Field-proven on the full ladder same-session: preview-missing → preview-only → preview-stale (source newer beyond the 60s skew tolerance) → recovery via the row's own next[] → live-stale → publish → fresh. Every verdict carried its executable recovery.

Lock

Locks in site-freshness.test.js now use the REAL /list shape (epoch-ms numbers): numeric normalization, and an end-to-end row proving preview-stale is detected when the numeric source timestamp beats the preview — with the admin snapshot present as the trap it used to fall into. Verified failing on old code; 609/609.
